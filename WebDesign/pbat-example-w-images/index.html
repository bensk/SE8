Designing the Circuit

We designed and built custom parts to use with an Arduino. Earlier in the unit, we used pre-built parts, but for our sound logger, we needed to build a microphone, an SD card holder, and a clock

Parts

Our Sound Logger consisted of three main components:

  1. Microphone This was the most important component, necessary to measure sound levels.
  2. SD Card We could have just logged data to a computer, but that would've required a computer near the sound logger in every room. Instead, we built an SD card reader, so that data could be stored on a removeable card.
  3. Clock This component was also not necessary, but was very useful in order to know what time a noise was happening. Otherwise, we would need to add up 10 second increments in order to get to the time that we wanted.

Data

Room May 18th Sound Levels

Our data indicates that the loudest class in Room 300 is 9th Grade Economics. This can be seen in the raised sound levels between 10:15AM and 11AM.

There were two very loud spikes- one at 9:45AM and one at 12:30PM. These were 10 times louder than any other noise observed during the day. I hypothesize that these were either a door slamming, or something directly impacting the microphone (which rested on a table at the front of the room)

Sources of Error

Our sound loggers were placed in five different classrooms. These rooms are in use for different periods throughout the day. Some rooms are empty for some periods, while other rooms are used the entire day. This will impact the average sound observed in each rooms, because empty rooms are significantly quieter than rooms with students.

Another possible source of error is damage or tampering with the sensors. Once they were placed in classrooms, they were inspected once a day, but frequently had to be replaced or fixed after suffering minor damage. This caused gaps in the data, which impacted our results.
